calmness · command reference
Command palette

Everything usually hidden in three levels of menus — now behind a single key.

Commands are grouped by the problem they solve: working with messages, conversation pace, group chats, organization, and quick actions without opening a chat.

Working with messages

Find, pin, defer, or forward — without losing focus on the conversation.

/find

Search across all conversations or within a specific chat.

/pin

Pin the last or a selected message.

/remind in 2 hours

Bring a message back to the top later, without keeping it in your head.

/forward

Send a message to another chat without manual copying.

Pace management

The decision about urgency stays with the person, not the interface.

/urgent

Mark your message as important before sending.

/not urgent

Explicitly remove the expectation of an immediate reply.

/silence for a day

Temporarily hide a chat from the active list.

/quiet hours

Turn on "do not disturb" for a chat or all conversations.

/close day

Mark incoming messages as processed for today.

Group chats

Less noise in the stream, more meaning on the other side.

/digest

A brief summary of missed messages by topic.

/slow mode

Limit the frequency of messages in a group.

/who read

A private list instead of public read receipts.

Organization

Conversations are an archive you can manage.

/archive

Remove a chat from the list while preserving history.

/rename

Change a chat or group name.

/export

Export your conversation history.

Quick actions

Without opening a chat — straight from the palette.

/status will reply later

Manually set your status, independent of auto-detection.

/send [name]: text

Write to another chat without switching windows.

More for the chat

New directions not yet in the palette

Delayed sending by default

A message doesn't go out immediately, but after a few seconds — you can catch and cancel an impulsive phrase before the other person sees it.

Context instead of "online"

The status describes what the person is doing ("driving", "on the go", "in focus"), not just the fact of presence — so you can better judge whether to expect a quick reply.

Silence room

A shared focus mode for the whole app: for a chosen time, all signals disappear, and afterward — a calm summary of what happened.

Defer for later

A gesture similar to "archive email" — the message moves to a separate quiet "read later" list, without creating the obligation to reply right now.

Draft together

In a group chat, you can collectively compose a single message before it goes out to everyone — useful for decisions where wording matters.
